FAQ

Being kind is like dancing...

...the more you practice, the better you get. This app helps you put more kindness in your life by spending a few moments each day meditating/reflecting/praying on it.

How does the app work?

The MTL CITY Kindness App sends you a daily quote, at a time you choose, about kindness and its positive effects on you and those around you.

The app helps you practice kindness by creating a few minutes of timed, silent meditation on kindness in your life - you decide how long.

Finding time to meditate/reflect/pray on kindness...

Here are a couple of suggestions if you haven't meditated before or are having trouble creating the habit of meditating/reflecting/praying:

  • Walk or sit to meditate/reflect/pray;
  • Start small - commit to just 2 minutes a day. You can do it for more if you feel good about it, but start by committing to something you can stick with.
  • Pick a trigger - you can use the app's timed, daily quote as a trigger or something like brushing your teeth or washing the dishes at supper to remind you to start.
  • Find a quiet spot - if you sit, sit comfortably on a pillow or a chair. If you walk, walk on a smooth, level surface, indoors or out. This isn't hiking or aerobics; your focus should be on quieting your mind and body.
  • Focus on your breath - set the timer, clear your mind and concentrate on your breathing - follow it in through your nostrils, your throat, your chest and belly. Sit or walk straight, keep your eyes open but looking at the ground in front of you with a soft focus.
  • End with kindness - as your meditation/reflection/prayer time ends, think about your day ahead and how watching for and doing kindness can be part of it.

Is that all there is to it?

Yup, it's that simple. Try meditating on kindness in your life for at least 2 minutes following the same trigger every day. Do this for a month and you'll have a daily kindness meditation/reflection/prayer habit. We think you'll also be a lot more self-aware and already practicing more kindness with the people around you. Kindness is good for - that's the message from religion, from psychology, and from neuroscience!*
